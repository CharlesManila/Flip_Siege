1. What you are playing

Flip-Siege is a team trick-taking siege game. Each physical card is a monster on one side and a tower on the other. Your team sieges with monsters face-up; the enemy defends with towers face-up.

Cards that do not match the led color earn team Resources by color. After each round you spend Resources at the Armory for repairs, reserve benching, and combat buffs. Cards you played to tricks rest one deal (cooldown — §6.2). Reduce the enemy castle to 0 HP to win.

One sentence: Win tricks → gain resources from off-color/trophies → buy Armory spikes → siege again.

2. Components & setup

What you need

  • 80 cards — 40 per team (same faces; different card backs for red vs blue team)
  • 2 castle HP tracks (dials or paper)
  • Resource tracker per team (green / blue / red / yellow + total cap)
  • Armory board or list (upgrade prices)
  • Team sheet — Permanent chosen, round buffs ticked (optional)

2v2 setup

  1. Partners sit together. Each team uses one 40-card deck (one back color).
  2. Agree starting castle HP and write it on both tracks (see §5).
  3. Agree follow rule: must-follow (standard) or may-follow (variant).
  4. Card cooldown is standard in v0.4 (played cards rest next deal, max 10 per team — §6.2).
  5. Trophies: auto if one qualifies or Block ≥ Assault; else pick one (see §11).
  6. Choose first sieging team (coin flip). Round = 1.
  7. Deal round 1 hands (see deal table in §6).

1v1 setup

Same as 2v2 except each player has a personal deck, a 7-card pyramid companion, and plays two cards per trick (hand + pyramid or reserve). See §6.1.

5. Goal & castle HP

Primary win: Reduce the enemy castle to 0 HP (siege victory) — in any round if damage is enough.

Pacing: At standard HP (60 must-follow / 68 may-follow, with cooldown), about half of games end in round 4 and half reach round 5+. A small share can end in round 3. Castles can reach 0 HP in any round.

Repair heals +2 HP but never above your starting HP.

High HP warning: Very long games can overemphasize round-5+ buff cycles and optional deck-exhaustion wins instead of early siege knockouts.

6. Round structure

Each match is a series of rounds. One round = deal → tricks → Armory.

Max tricks = cards dealt − 1. The round usually ends when everyone is down to 1 card (often one trick before the cap).

6.1 — Steps each round (2v2)

  1. Deal — Deal the round’s hand size to each player; remainder → reserve. (Exclude resting cards — §6.2.)
  2. Alternating siege — Trick 1: team that sieges first this round (Red/Blue token). Trick 2: other team sieges. Trick 3: first team again, and so on. Within the sieging team, partners alternate who leads.
  3. Next round — Swap which team sieges trick 1 (if Red sieged first in round 1, Blue sieges first in round 2).
  4. Calamity (round 3 and every round 5+) — Last trick is a deck siege; both teams defend (§14). No trophies.
  5. Round end — Any player has exactly 1 card after a trick, or max tricks were played.
  6. Armory — Each team makes 2 purchases (§12). Played cards become resting next deal (§6.2).
Example (round 1, 7 dealt / 6 tricks): After 5 tricks everyone has 2 cards; after trick 6 everyone has 1 card → round ends (cap was 6).

6.2 — Card cooldown (tower & monster rest)

After each round, cards your team played to tricks are resting:

  • They are not dealt in the next round (neither to hand nor reserve).
  • They return to the living pool for the round after that (same as “skip one deal”).
  • Cap: At most 10 resting cards per team (if you played more, only the 10 most recent plays rest).
  • Cards not played (left in hand or reserve) are not affected. Cards you played (including off-color cards that generated resources) do rest.
  • Armory: spending resources does not add new rest.

This rotates which monsters and towers show up — plan around colors that are “on rest” next deal.

Deal safeguard (no short deals)

Before each deal, if your team cannot fill every player’s hand from the living pool, the game auto-relieves pressure in order:

  1. Resting cards return to the pool until hands can be filled.
  2. Cooldown-marked reserve cards return to the pool only after they sit out one deal.

Deal safeguard keeps rounds playable in low-pool edge cases by auto-relieving resting/bench pressure before dealing.

6.3 — 1v1 companion pyramid

Layout: 1 face-down, 2 face-down, 4 face-up. Each trick: siege hand → siege slot → defense hand → defense slot. Slot = one card from uncovered pyramid or top of reserve (not both). Flip covered pyramid cards when uncovered.

7. Tricks step by step

Each trick = 4 cards (2v2). Roles are fixed for that trick: one team sieges, one defends.

  1. Sieging team alternates each trick (odd trick: team that sieges first this round; even trick: other team).
  2. Leader (sieging team; partners alternate who leads) plays monster-up. Led color = that monster’s color.
  3. Defender A plays tower-up.
  4. Siege partner plays monster-up.
  5. Defender B plays tower-up.
  6. Resolve Assault, Block, damage, resources (§10), trophies (§11) — except on calamity (§14).
  7. Check round end / game end (§15).

If a player has no cards in hand when they would play, skip them (rare after short deal).

8. Follow color

Must-follow (official)

If you have any card of led color in hand (on the face you are allowed to play), you must play a led-color card.

May-follow (optional variant)

If you have led color, you may instead play an off-color card for resources (still the correct face: monster on siege, tower on defense). Use 68 HP in 2v2 instead of 60. All other rules the same.

Void: No led color in hand → play any card (often off-color for resource gain).

9. Assault, Block & damage

Assault (siege team)

Sum monster ranks of led-color monsters played by the sieging team, plus Armory bonuses on the first siege trick of the round (War Drums, Siege Breaker, etc.), plus Permanent bonuses (§13).

Block (defense team)

Sum tower ranks of led-color towers played by the defending team, plus first-defense-trick buffs (Boiling Oil, Iron Curtain), plus Permanent bonuses.

Damage

Damage = Assault − Block (minimum 0). Apply to the defending team’s castle. There is no rule that castles must stop at 1 HP in early rounds — if Assault overwhelms Block, the castle can fall.

Combat tier (round 5+ massacre)

Add this tier to War Drums, Boiling Oil, Siege Breaker, Iron Curtain, and some Permanent effects:

R1–4: +0 R5: +5 R6: +10 R7+: +11…

Example: Siege Breaker (+9 base) on round 6 first siege trick → +9 + 10 = +19 Assault before cards.

Worked example (2v2)

Lead green. Siege: Goblin 3 + Goblin 1 → Assault 4. Defense: Bow 2 + off-color Orc → Block 2. Damage 2. Orc gives defense team resources = ⌊4/2⌋ = 2.

10. Resources (off-color + trophies)

Flip-Siege now uses a resource tracker (no physical stash cards, no borrowed trophies).

  • Off-color play: gain ⌊rank / 2⌋ resources in that card's color.
  • Trophy reward: gain ⌊monster rank / 2⌋ resources in the trophy card's color.
  • Global cap: each team can hold at most 28 total resources across all colors.
  • Overflow: income beyond the cap is lost.
  • Spend: Armory costs still pay by color (green/blue/red/yellow pools).

11. Trophy rewards

After resolving damage, check trophies:

  1. Look at enemy siege monsters that contributed led-color Assault > 0.
  2. If your team’s Block ≥ that monster’s rank (printed monster rank on the card), that monster is eligible.
  3. How many you take:
    • One eligible → take it automatically.
    • Several eligible and your team’s Block ≥ resolved Assault (after buffs, before damage) → take all eligible monsters.
    • Several eligible but Block < Assault → defending team picks one.
  4. No card is taken. Gain resource points for the chosen trophy: ⌊monster rank / 2⌋ in that card's color.

No borrow lock: trophies do not remove cards from the enemy deck in this ruleset.

12. Armory (worker draft)

After each round, run a worker draft: four players pick in trick-1 order for the upcoming round (siege lead → defender → siege partner → defender). Each pick: place a worker on one color station and buy, or pass (worker leaves the board).

  • Green — repair (scaled green cost, choose HP healed).
  • Yellow — War Drums or Siege Breaker (round 5+).
  • Blue — Boiling Oil or Iron Curtain (round 5+). One blue worker globally per draft.
  • Red — send 1–3 reserve cards to next-deal cooldown (you choose which). One red worker globally per draft.
  • No Vault station in the live game.
  • Cannot return to the same color station you just left on your previous pick.
  • Max one red depth tradeoff per team per Armory.
  • Permanent (round 4+): may be bought instead of a station pick when eligible (one per team per game).

Royal Sigil — stacking order

Cards with monster ranks 1–3 of your Sigil color are elite. When that color is led:

  1. Start with the face rank (monster rank on siege, tower rank on defense).
  2. Add combat tier (+0 rounds 1–4, +5 round 5, +10 round 6, +11 round 7…).
  3. On your team’s first siege trick of the round (siege only): add active War Drums and/or Siege Breaker (full scaled values) to each elite’s base before doubling.
  4. On your team’s first defense trick of the round: same for Boiling Oil / Iron Curtain on elite tower ranks.
  5. Double that total. Early-round combat trim still applies after doubling.

If a Sigil elite contributed Assault (or Block) on that trick, those round buffs are not added again at trick level — avoids double-counting.

You cannot combine Sigil with Warlord’s Crest / Color Mastery (one Permanent per team). Pair Sigil with round buys (Drums, Breaker) for late-game spikes.

Elite example (Royal Sigil green, round 6)

Your team has War Drums and Siege Breaker. First siege trick, lead green. You play elite Goblin 2 (partner plays Goblin 10):

Elite base = 2 + tier 10 + Drums 12 + Breaker 19 = 43 → 86 Assault from that card (before trim). Partner adds 10; trick-level Drums/Breaker are not added again because the elite absorbed them.

Elites do not auto-win tricks.

14. Calamity (round 3 & round 5+)

On round 3 and every round 5 and later, the last trick is a calamity — a deck siege that hits both castles. Announce at the start of the round: “Calamity round — last trick is a deck siege.”

Assault (from reserves)

  1. Flip the top reserve monster from each deck in rotation (Team A, then Team B, then A… across the match).
  2. First card sets led color and contributes its monster rank.
  3. Second card adds its monster rank to assault (same led color for defense).

Defense (both teams)

Each team defends like a normal trick: every player plays one card tower-up (must-follow led color if you can; off-color → resources). No trophies during calamity. Assault = sum of both flipped monster ranks (no round trim on that total). Each castle takes damage = Assault + underprep penalty − that team’s Block (min 0).

After calamity resolves, the two flipped reserve monsters are not played — return them to the top of the same reserves. They do not enter card cooldown (only tower cards played from hand do).

Reserve prep

  • Prepared: After those flips, your reserve still has ≥9 cards → bonus Block (+1 per card above 8, cap +7) plus add ⅓ of the best tower rank among your top 3 reserve cards.
  • Underprepared: Each card below 9 in your reserve → +1 extra damage; empty reserve → +5 more.

Keep reserve depth going into the last trick; don’t rely only on hand towers.

Fast block math (table)

  1. Start from prep block (reserve wall + depth bonus).
  2. Add each defender's led-color tower block (off-color = 0 block).
  3. Add first-defense buffs if active (Boiling Oil / Iron Curtain).
  4. Total block = prep + cards + buffs.
  5. Damage = Assault + underprep − total block (minimum 0).

15. When rounds & games end

Round ends when (either)

  • Any player has exactly 1 card in hand after a trick → go to Armory (no more tricks this round).
  • Your team has played max tricks for this round → Armory.

Important: If everyone hits 1 card before the calamity trick on a calamity round, you skip calamity that round.

Match ends when (either)

  • Siege victory: A castle reaches 0 HP (any round).
  • Deck exhaustion (optional): From round 6+, if any player has ≤ half the round’s deal size in hand after a trick (e.g. ≤5 when deal is 10), the game ends — highest castle HP wins. Default tournament rules often skip this.

Short deal

If the living pool cannot fill all hands (common with cooldown, purge, and benched cards), deal as many cards as possible from what exists. Some players may get fewer cards or zero.

16. FAQ & edge cases

Can I buy Repair and a Permanent in the same Armory?
Yes, if you have 2 purchases. Permanent uses one.
Can I buy multiple Red depth tradeoffs in one visit?
No. Max one Red purchase per team per Armory visit.
What if reserve is empty on Red?
You cannot buy Red. The purchase is illegal.
Does Purge remove cards currently in reserve/hand?
Yes. Great Purge removes all 10 cards of that color from your deck state when it resolves.
Can the enemy deal a card I “won” as a trophy?
Yes. Trophies give resource points only; no card is captured or borrowed.
Who sieges trick 1 each round?
Alternate by round (Red trick 1 in round 1 → Blue trick 1 in round 2). Within the round, siege alternates every trick; partners on the sieging team alternate who leads.
Do off-color cards in a trick count for Assault/Block?
No. Only led-color ranks count. Off-color gives resources only.
How much resource do I get from off-color or trophies?
⌊rank/2⌋ (1→0, 10→5). Team total is capped at 28.
Is there a hand size 12?
No. Cap is 11 from round 5 onward.
Legacy Gold / Stash-card rules?
Not used in the live game. Official v0.5 uses the resource tracker plus cooldown.
Can a castle die in round 1–3?
Yes. There is no “stuck at 1 HP” rule. At standard HP, early knockouts are uncommon (~5–8% in round 3) but possible if one team spikes Assault.
Why not the old “no kill before round 4” rule?
Removed so sieges feel real every round. Pacing is tuned with 60 HP (must) / 68 HP (may), card cooldown, and calamity on round 3 & every round 5+.
Do resource points go to cooldown?
No. Cooldown only applies to cards played to tricks.
Do resting cards go in the reserve pile?
No. Resting is separate from reserve. Reserve = undealt this round only. Resting = played last round, excluded from the next deal everywhere.
